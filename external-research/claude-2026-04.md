# External citations and coverage of Yue Zhao's research tools

Yue Zhao's open-source tools — particularly **PyOD** and **TrustLLM** — have achieved remarkable penetration across industry platforms, global policy assessments, educational materials, and international media in at least six languages. The most consequential citation is TrustLLM's role as a **core safety benchmark in the Future of Life Institute's AI Safety Index**, which directly grades Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta, xAI, and DeepSeek and has been covered by Reuters, The Atlantic, TIME, Bloomberg, Fortune, The Guardian, and Nature. PyOD, meanwhile, has become the de facto standard Python anomaly detection library, with verified enterprise adoption at Databricks and Ericsson, inclusion in published textbooks, and dedicated courses reaching millions of learners worldwide.

## Conclusion

Yue Zhao's research ecosystem has achieved a rare dual impact: **PyOD has become infrastructure** — embedded in enterprise platforms (Databricks), telecom networks (Ericsson), educational curricula (DataCamp's 19M learners, Springer textbooks, Udemy courses), and practitioner workflows across at least six languages. **TrustLLM has become policy-grade** — its integration into the FLI AI Safety Index means it directly shapes how the world's leading AI companies are publicly graded on safety, with those grades amplified through Reuters, Nature, Bloomberg, The Atlantic, and Fortune.

The coverage gap is notable: no citations were found in official government documents (NIST, EU AI Act, UK AISI), no direct references in OpenAI/Anthropic/Google/Meta model cards, no patent citations in USPTO/Google Patents, and no coverage from tier-1 tech publications (TechCrunch, Wired, MIT Tech Review). The newer tools — DoxBench, agent-audit, Aegis — are too recent for external coverage beyond their conference proceedings. TDC, while published in Nature Chemical Biology, lacks standalone external media coverage that explicitly names Yue Zhao. These represent the frontier where adoption has not yet matched the tools' academic impact.

**Structure (two program elements, intentionally small for the inaugural year):**

1. **Rising Star Job Talks** — selective, requires CFP submission. Accepted speakers receive the *IEEE BigData 2026 Future Faculty Rising Star* designation, listed on the conference website (precedents: MIT EECS Rising Stars, UChicago Data Science Rising Stars).
2. **Faculty AMA Panel** — closed Q&A with 4-5 senior faculty, open by sign-up to academic-job-market candidates registered for the conference. Rising Stars are auto-registered.

**Timeline (Rising Star track):** Submission Oct 24 (= main paper notification day, a memorable date) → Notification Nov 7 (lands before the typical IEEE early-bird window) → Confirmation Nov 21 → Bio/photo/abstract Dec 5 → Conference Dec 14-17.

**Six open questions with my recommended defaults:**

1. **AMA scope and panelists** — closed to academic-job-market candidates; cochairs invite 4-5 panelists across career stages and institution types.
2. **Industry research-scientist eligibility** — academic-track only for year one; expand if the OC sees demand.
3. **Deadline calibration** — Oct 24 / Nov 7 / Nov 21 / Dec 5; AMA sign-up closes Dec 5.
4. **CyberChair Rising Star track route** — needs a route registered (same pattern as Tutorials and PhD Forum); AMA sign-up via Google form linked from the conference website.
5. **Travel support for Rising Stars** — three options on the table (self-pay / partial stipend / reduced registration); please weigh in.
6. **Recording policy for Rising Star talks** — no recording for year one as a safe default; AMA is never recorded under any policy.

If the defaults look reasonable, please leave a `+1` comment in the doc on each. If you want a different choice, please leave the alternative directly in the doc so we can converge.

Target: lock the answers and a posting-ready version by **May 15**, so the call goes live in early June and candidates have ~5 months of lead time before the October 24 deadline.

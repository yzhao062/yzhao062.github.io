⏰ Two days left. The First Workshop on Agent Skills has its paper deadline on May 1, 2026 (AoE): https://www.agentskills-workshop.org/

The workshop is co-located with the inaugural ACM Conference on AI and Agentic Systems (CAIS 2026), May 26-29 in San Jose. CAIS is ACM's new venue for compound AI systems and agent architectures, distinct from the language-model scaffolds being built on top of today's foundation models. First-edition conferences mean early citation traction and a community still being shaped.

If you have work on procedural knowledge for LLM agents, the workshop is the right place. Topics in scope:

🛠️ Agent Skills and Applications (skill design, decomposition, agent behaviors)
📊 Evaluation and Benchmarking (capability assessment, task suites, leaderboards)
🛡️ Safety and Supply Chain (agent attribution, malicious skill detection, sandboxing)
📈 Improvement and Learning (skill discovery, transfer, RL-based skill optimization)

Why submit (and why attend):
✨ Short paper track is 4 pages plus references; full track is 9 pages. OpenReview, double-blind.
📜 Non-archival: authors retain the right to publish the work elsewhere afterward, so submitting here does not lock the paper out of a later venue. Work-in-progress, position papers, and negative results are all welcome.
🎤 Keynotes from Dawn Song (UC Berkeley), Manling Li (Northwestern), Ross Taylor (General Reasoning), Kanav Garg (Google DeepMind), and Yu Su (Ohio State).
📍 Workshop: May 26, 2026, San Jose, full-day program. CAIS main program runs May 27-29.
